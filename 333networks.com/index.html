Welcome!

333networks wrote a masterserver for all your legacy games from scratch, monitors UT(demo) servers and provides you with server information and UT(demo) resources! Keep an eye on your UT server, read our tutorials and join our community! At 333networks, we try to keep improving wherever we can. We do this as a hobby, for recreation, out of curiousity, and to make new friends.

Update

We just updated to a new version of our masterserver (17 nov 2016). If you encounter problems, please email us on info@333networks.com! More information below.

Development (17/11-2016)

So what's going on? Why haven't I heard anything about 333networks lately?

We have been working hard on a version of our site! We are now testing our newest version of the masterserver, version 2.2.4. This masterserver is stronger, faster and better than any before. It can handle more games and more users at the same time, and is now used for all 333networks information services. And there's country flags! CHECK IT OUT!

In the upcoming months we will be working on the user experience and style, new features and statistics. Of course, you guys (m/f) know that this site may have bugs and/or inconsistencies. When you figure out that something does not work, or does not work as you expected, it would be really appreciated if you could report this to us. You can do this in the following ways:

Email Darkelarious on darkelarious[at]333networks.com and report what you consider incorrect or disfunctional. Please mention that it's about the "dev" site and attach screenshots if applicable.

Join us on irc: irc.synirc.net #333networks and explain what the problem is. Be aware that we all live in different timezones and have full-time jobs. It may take a long, long time before you get a response. Please be patient, eventually we'll get to reading your report!

Your efforts will be greatly appreciated!

Killing Floor

Admin Guide

Since the first Unreal Tournament servers were started, people wanted to play for pleasure. To make the game more enjoyable for all players, some player rules were written. But later on, some admins did not act as responsible and respectable as the players. Therefore, a second set of rules was written to remind admins how to behave and act honorable. These rules were combined into an admin guide.
