Characters[edit]

Good guys[edit]

T.J. "Hiryuu" Chastain: Hero of the game and just the man for the job, more or less. Since the real Hiryuu really likes Ranma 1/2, his character is also cursed. Yawn worthy but that's life for ya. He wears an EliteMap shirt much like the one I'm wearing right now and can dress up for specific missions.

Rick "Trasher" Trimble: One of the resistance leaders and resident Aussie. He's the one dishing out the missions but mostly just lounges around, watching hentai on TV. He wears a bright green jacket and -another- EliteMap shirt (he designed it after all).

Kyoufu Kawa: Resident tentacle demon, provider of entertainment for the resistance and general off-color bastard. Is actually no higher in command than Trasher, just seems to be. Wears a Chinese shirt and leather jacket but that doesn't matter 'cuz he's off-color anyway.

Bad guys[edit]

Sebastian De La Hoya: The man behind the noodle conspiracy. Rich, smart, and not to be fucked with. Short black hair, buisness fair. short, stocky, and fit.

Michael Crane: Runs an underground Ramen ring. Not a friendly, or paticuarly handsome gentleman. Looks quite brutish.

The Anti-Rodent: A strange entity, seems to be the exact opposite to Kawa. As such, expect the fur to fly when the two meet.

Kawa: Why would Anti be listed under "bad guys" anyway? I'm evil, so he must be good!

Outsider: This figure works outside of all the groups, fiddling with them all equally.

Common Enemies[edit]

Clones: Genetic Multiplications of a past antagonist called Ninja AE, who generally was indecipherable. Weak alone, but you never find them alone...

Groups[edit]

The Organisation: For truth, justice, and stir-fry, this intrepid, if somewhat amateur, group work to end the government's somewhat bizarre noodle conspiracy, and bring them back to being safe, legal foodstuffs. Unfortunately, they likely have more on the menu... (in the form of illegalising all other foods!)

Kawa: How would one neutralize the psi-powers of noodles anyway?

kins: eating them raw.

Kawa: Ah. Crunchy.
