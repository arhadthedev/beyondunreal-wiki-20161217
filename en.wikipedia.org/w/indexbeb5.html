IMPORTANT NOTE: Most educators and professionals do not consider it appropriate to use tertiary sources such as encyclopedias as a sole source for any information—citing an encyclopedia as an important reference in footnotes or bibliographies may result in censure or a failing grade. Wikipedia articles should be used for background information, as a reference for correct terminology and search terms, and as a starting point for further research.

As with any community-built reference, there is a possibility for error in Wikipedia's content—please check your facts against multiple sources and read our disclaimers for more information.

Citation styles for "Wiki"

APA style

Wiki. (2016, December 2). In Wikipedia, The Free Encyclopedia. Retrieved 07:20, December 2, 2016, from https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Wiki&oldid=752613214

MLA style

Wikipedia contributors. "Wiki." Wikipedia, The Free Encyclopedia. Wikipedia, The Free Encyclopedia, 2 Dec. 2016. Web. 2 Dec. 2016.

MHRA style

Wikipedia contributors, 'Wiki', Wikipedia, The Free Encyclopedia, 2 December 2016, 07:20 UTC, <https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Wiki&oldid=752613214> [accessed 2 December 2016]

Chicago style

Wikipedia contributors, "Wiki," Wikipedia, The Free Encyclopedia, https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Wiki&oldid=752613214 (accessed December 2, 2016).

CBE/CSE style

Wikipedia contributors. Wiki [Internet]. Wikipedia, The Free Encyclopedia; 2016 Dec 2, 07:20 UTC [cited 2016 Dec 2]. Available from: https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Wiki&oldid=752613214.

Bluebook style

Wiki, https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Wiki&oldid=752613214 (last visited Dec. 2, 2016).

AMA style

Wikipedia contributors. Wiki. Wikipedia, The Free Encyclopedia. December 2, 2016, 07:20 UTC. Available at: https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Wiki&oldid=752613214. Accessed December 2, 2016.

BibTeX entry

 @misc{ wiki:xxx,
   author = "Wikipedia",
   title = "Wiki --- Wikipedia{,} The Free Encyclopedia",
   year = "2016",
   url = "https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Wiki&oldid=752613214",
   note = "[Online; accessed 2-December-2016]"
 }

When using the LaTeX package url (\usepackage{url} somewhere in the preamble), which tends to give much more nicely formatted web addresses, the following may be preferred: 

 @misc{ wiki:xxx,
   author = "Wikipedia",
   title = "Wiki --- Wikipedia{,} The Free Encyclopedia",
   year = "2016",
   url = "\url{https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Wiki&oldid=752613214}",
   note = "[Online; accessed 2-December-2016]"
 }
